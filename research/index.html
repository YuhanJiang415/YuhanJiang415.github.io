Research

Here are my research interests in the field of Geospatial Data Science, which encompass geospatial artificial intelligence (GeoAI) algorithm development, spatiotemporal data analytics, cartography and geo-visualizations.

Map retrieval intention recognition

  • Drawing on relevant feedback data, this research aims to enhance the retrieval accuracy and assists the discovery and utilization of resources in an intention-driven way. In my undergraduate thesis, titled "WMS Layer Retrieval Intention Recognition Considering Map Features and Semantic Attributes", this issue was addressed with DBSCAN. [PDF]

  • Currently, the hypergraph segmentation method is being employed to further optimize this research.

Spatial analysis of COVID-19

  • This study focuses on quantifying the spatial differences of COVID-19 cases in Wuhan utilizing social media data (Weibo) [Data]. To achieve this objective, we employed Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) and Geographically Weighted Regression (GWR) techniques to explore the spatial distribution patterns of COVID-19 cases in Wuhan. Furthermore, we conducted a correlation analysis to investigate the potential relationships between the distribution of COVID-19 cases and environmental factors. [PDF]

Simulation of grassland desertification

  • I utilize Netlogo to comprehensively simulate the process of grassland desertification, considering environmental, climatic, and ecological factors. Four major models have been established, including a weather prediction model based on Markov chains, a grass growth model, a model for the life cycles of sheep and wolves, and a desert expansion model.
