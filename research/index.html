Map retrieval intent recognition

With the improvement of geography and geoscience data collection and sharing technologies, a large number of various geographic information resources are available on the Internet. While providing abundant data resources for geoscientific research and application, it also brings great challenges to accurate resource retrieval and discovery. Drawing on relevant user feedback data and geographic semantics guidance, this research project aims to enhance the retrieval accuracy and assist the discovery and utilization of online geographic resources in an intention-driven way.

  • Currently, the hypergraph segmentation method I proposed has been employed to further optimize this research. It enables the exploration of potential associations among attributes of user-related positive feedback samples, explicitly describing compound semantics within the intent. At the same time, this method helps uncover hidden intents and addresses the issue of incomplete intent expression due to the miss of metadata in some feedback samples, thereby supporting precise and robust retrieval of various geographic information resources. [PDF]

    (1) An intent representation model for expressing the intent of retrieving geographic information resources has been designed, featuring three-layer nested intent-subintent-dimension components.

    (2) By considering user feedback samples as vertices and the synonymous and hierarchical relationships among sample attributes as edges, multiple hypergraph construction schemes with varying degrees of semantic generalization are generated.

    (3) A segmentation cost metric is defined to select the optimal hypergraph construction and hypergraph segmentation schemes, resulting in multiple sub-hypergraphs that may express the same retrieval sub-intent.

    (4) Sub-intents are extracted from each sub-hypergraph, and then, evaluate their confidence levels, followed by the merging of all sub-intents into a single intent.

Spatial analysis of COVID-19

The lack of detailed COVID-19 cases at a fine spatial resolution restricts the investigation of spatial disparities of its attack rate. Here, we collected nearly one thousand self-reported cases from a social media platform (Weibo) during the early stage of COVID-19 epidemic in Wuhan, China [Data]. We used kernel density estimation (KDE) to explore spatial disparities of epidemic intensity and adopted a geographically weighted regression (GWR) model to quantify influences of population dynamics, transportation, and social interactions on COVID-19 epidemic. Results show that self-reported COVID-19 cases are concentrated in commercial centres and populous residential areas. Blocks with higher population density, higher ageing rate, more metro stations, more main roads, and more commercial point-of-interests (POIs) have a higher density of COVID-19 cases. These five explanatory variables explain 76% variance of self-reported cases using an OLS model. Commercial POIs have the strongest influence, which increases COVID-19 cases by 28% with one standard deviation increase. The GWR model performs better than OLS model with the adjusted R2 of 0.96. Spatial heterogeneities of coefficients in the GWR model show that influencing factors play different roles in diverse communities. We further discussed potential implications for a healthy city and urban planning for the sustainable development of cities. [PDF]

(1) We quantify spatial disparities of COVID-19 epidemic within Wuhan using social media data.

(2) Self-reported COVID-19 cases concentrated in commercial centres and populous residential areas.

(3) Population dynamics, transportation, and social interactions largely determine the epidemic.

(4) The idea of a healthy city is highly recommended to be incorporated into urban planning.

Simulation of grassland desertification

Guiding scientific grazing, strengthening the prevention and control of grassland desertification, and achieving both ecological and economic benefits are of great significance for grassland management. Empirical summary-based prevention and control methods for grassland desertification cannot effectively predict the progress of desertification from a global perspective. In this research, We primarily concentrated on ecology and environment of grasslands, and have established four models based on NetLogo simulation platform to simulate the process of grassland desertification. These models include a weather prediction model based on Markov chains, a grass growth model, a life-cycle model for sheep and wolves, and an expansion model for deserts. By using remote sensing images of Erenhot Banner in Inner Mongolia as experimental data, we simulate the process of grassland desertification and predict the characteristics of desertification in this region. The results show that the ecological carrying capacity of the area is very fragile, and it is recommended that relevant government departments restrict grazing, and take timely measures to control the desertification. At the same time, afforestation is suggested in areas where desertification has already occurred to slow down the process of desertification. [PDF] [Code]
